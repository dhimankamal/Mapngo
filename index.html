What is MAP

Mental Health Action by People (MAP)

is an NGO that aims to promote wellbeing and quality of life of fellowmen by proactive interventions in community Mental Health. The MAP is registered as a charitable organization (TVM/TC/289/2014). It was inaugurated by the famous social activist and poet Smt. Sugatha Kumari in the presence of dignitaries, Sree O Rajagopal (then union minister) , Sree A Sambath (member of parliament) and Sree K Muralidharan (member of legislative assembly)

Our Projects

Current Projects

My Buddy TextLine

This crisis TextLine project has been commenced to address two major issues:

  • Stress, anxiety and depression due to Covid-19 scenario
  • To address the suicidal behavior of children, adolescents and adults due to crises in life

Current Projects

Just Listening

Just Listening is a community-counseling platform. This is a new paradigm in counseling that aims to connect and empower the needy segment of our community beyond the four walls. More than 500 Mental Health professionals including psychiatrist, social workers, counselors and psychologists have been trained in “Just Listening” counseling. The trained volunteers are waiting to role out their services in the community

Current Projects

Resilient youth

Resilient youth is an online resilient building toolkit developed by the MAP team. This project aims to promote resilience among the youth primary prevention perspective to address positive mental health promotion among the youth

Our Past Work

Jyothis

Jyothis is about providing psychosocial care and support to the inmates of central prison in Thiruvananthapuram district, where in daily counseling support was provided to the inmates by a team of trained Mental Health professionals including counselors, psychologists and clinical psychologists. More than 500 inmates have been served during one year. Along with psychosocial care, a psychosocial profiling of the inmates has also been taken up

Our Past Work

Counseling and education for students

We have been providing individual and group counseling and also information education communication programs to the high school and higher secondary school students. The objective of this empowerment projects is to prevent substance abuse among the students community. This project helped the students to facilitate their academic achievements by providing facilitative environment and incentive system. More than 100 schools, mostly in coastal and rural areas, have been covered

Our Past Work

Disaster management against floods

We supported disaster management by providing psychosocial care to the flood affected victims in five districts. The MAP team in association with ‘Smile Again Kerala’, a project of US based Keralites, continued the psychosocial care and support during the rehabilitation phase in the temporary camps and households

Our Past Work

Training

Regular continuing education in clinical and rehabilitation psychology as well as mental health have been organized for the mental health professionals regularly. More than 1500 professionals in mental health have been trained in this regard
